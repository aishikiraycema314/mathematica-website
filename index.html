1.2: Data Types:

Core:

1. Integer: Whole numbers with no size limit, automatically handling arbitrary precision.
Example: 1, 2, 7
2.  Real: Floating-point numbers, covering both machine-precision and arbitrary-precision decimals.
Example: 6.023, 3.14
3. Rational: Exact fractions written explicitly as numerator/denominator.
Example:
4. Complex: Numbers containing an imaginary part, represented by the capital symbol I.
Example: 3 + 4I
5. String: Text characters wrapped in double quotes
Example:  “hello”
6. Symbol: Named variables, functions, or constants
Example: x, Pi

1.3: Commands for Basic Arithmetic

Mathematica works much like a calculator for basic arithmetic. Just use the +, –, *, and / keys on the keyboard for addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. As an alternative to typing *, you can multiply two numbers by leaving a space between them (the × symbol will automatically be inserted when you leave a space between two numbers).
